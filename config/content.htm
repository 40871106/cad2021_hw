Solidworks drawing

origin:

In 1993, founder Jon Hirschtick recruited a team of engineers to make 3D CAD software easier to use. Hirschtick raised $1 million from members of the famous MIT Blackjack team to start his new business. The goal is to build a team based onWindows platform is easy to use and affordable software . It really was revolutionary at the time, when all other popular CAD systems are in the Unix build on . After several years of development, the initial version of SolidWorks has finally arrived...

introduce:

The original version was released in 1995, and its features are as follows.

1. Compared with other CAD systems at 18,000 USD, the price of SolidWorks is only 4,000 USD.


2. The software is easier to use: Compared with other CAD software on the market, users only need 3 months of experience to get started.

  A large part of the ease of use is that it uses a modern and well-known Windows interface.


3. The visualization of the model is unprecedented. The architectural function in the well-lit shadow environment is in sharp contrast with the traditional modeling space.

  The traditional modeling space only displays the wire frame under the black background. The similar FeatureManager that we are now familiar with is used to display the construction history of the model.

4. Parts, assemblies and drawings are available from the beginning. The user cannot apply mates in the component, but the component can still be placed in place.

Solve space drawing

origin:

A brief comment and interview with developers was published on Libre Graphics World in 2013. This review praised SolveSpace for its small executable file, advanced constraint solver, and range of output formats . The same comment pointed out some shortcomings, mainly its slow and limited processing of Boolean values, and the lack of native Linux support . However, native Linux support has since been added.

introduce:

- 3D Modeling Part - Boolean stretching and rotation (and / Derivative) operation to draw


- Modeling Part 2D - drawn as a single piece part, and exported as DXF, PDF, or the SVG; using complex 3D assembled verified

 Prepare CAM data for waterjet or laser cutting machine to export two-dimensional vector diagram; or generate STEP or STL to import to a third party

 CAM software
- mechanism design-use constraint solvers to simulate plane or space links, including pins, balls or sliding joints
- plane and solid geometry-replace manually solved trigonometric functions and spreadsheets with real-time dimensional drawings
